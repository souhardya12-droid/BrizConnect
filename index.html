🚌 Transport

Getting around Bristol

Everything you need to know — buses, trains, taxis. Real talk, no spin.

🚌
First Bus – City Routes
Key routes to Clifton, Stoke Bishop & city centre. The U1/U2 are your main UoB routes — get a student bus pass to save big.
⚠️ Buses run late & cancel often — leave 10 min early, always. Seriously.
First Bus routes & passes →
🚂
Trains – Temple Meads & Clifton Down
Bristol Temple Meads is the main hub. Clifton Down station is handy for the Downs area. Use 16–25 Railcard + Trainline for cheapest fares.
🎟️ Book advance tickets on Trainline for big savings vs walking up.
Trainline →   Travel West →
🚖
Taxi & Uber
Uber is widely available across Bristol. Licensed black cabs available at Temple Meads. Arrow Cars is a popular local firm — often cheaper late night.
💡 Split Uber with flatmates after nights out — saves everyone.
Uber Bristol →
🚲
Cycling & Scooting
Bristol has decent cycle lanes. Voi & Tier e-scooters are available with student discounts. Cycling from Redland to campus takes ~15 mins.
🔒 Invest in a good D-lock — bike theft is common in the city.
Voi e-scooters →
🗺️
Key Campus Connections
Main campus: U1/U2 buses. Langford (vet): X5 from Temple Meads. Frenchay (UWE): no.17 bus. Free UoB shuttle from Stoke Bishop halls.
📱 Download the First Bus app — live tracking is a lifesaver.
UoB getting around guide →
🅿️
Parking in Bristol
City centre parking is expensive. Resident permit zones cover most student areas. If your accommodation has parking — use it. Park & Ride from the edges is cheaper.
🚗 Most UoB halls don't allow cars in 1st year. Check your contract!
Bristol parking info →
